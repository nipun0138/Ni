Off-Page SEO Strategies for Authority Growth

Strengthen your reputation and visibility through signals that support search trust.

Published: October 2024 Reading time: 7 minutes

What Is Off-Page SEO

Off-page SEO refers to actions taken outside your website to improve rankings. This primarily includes backlinks, but also brand mentions, citations, and reputation signals.

Brand Mentions

Search engines track brand mentions across the web. Unlinked mentions can still pass some authority. The more your brand is mentioned, the more trust search engines place in you.

Citations and Local Signals

Consistent business name, address, and phone number (NAP) citations improve local rankings. List your business on directories relevant to your industry.

Review Management

Customer reviews are both a trust signal and content that search engines index. Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews. Respond professionally to all reviews.

Social Signals

While social media links don't directly pass authority, social signals indicate content quality and brand strength. Maintain an active social presence.

Digital PR

Earn mentions and coverage in news outlets and relevant publications. Digital PR builds authority and generates brand awareness.

Conclusion

Off-page SEO builds your brand authority and trust signals. Focus on earning quality mentions, building citations, and managing your reputation.
