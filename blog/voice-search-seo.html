Voice Search SEO Optimization

A guide to adapting your content for spoken queries and assistant-driven search.

Published: August 2024 Reading time: 6 minutes

Voice Search is Growing Rapidly

Smart speakers, mobile assistants, and voice-enabled devices are becoming increasingly common. Voice search queries are different from typed searches—they tend to be longer, more conversational, and question-based.

Optimizing for voice search means adapting your content strategy to capture these growing voice queries.

Featured Snippets Optimization

Voice search often pulls answers from featured snippets. Optimize your content to win featured snippets by answering specific questions clearly and concisely.

Use FAQ schema markup to mark up your questions and answers, making it easier for search engines to extract answers.

Question-Based Keywords

Optimize for keywords that start with "how," "what," "why," "when," "where," and "who." These are natural voice search queries.

Create content that comprehensively answers these question keywords.

Natural Language Content

Voice search is conversational, so write more naturally. Use contractions, shorter sentences, and conversational language. Answer questions as if you're speaking to someone.

Local Optimization

Many voice searches are location-based ("near me" queries). Ensure your Google Business Profile is complete and optimized.

Conclusion

Voice search is a growing channel. By optimizing for conversational keywords and featured snippets, you'll capture this growing traffic.
