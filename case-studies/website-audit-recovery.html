Client Profile

A mid-size B2B software company with 8 years of strong organic visibility. Had consistently ranked for important keywords and generated significant revenue from organic traffic. Suddenly experienced a 60% drop in organic traffic after a Google algorithm update.

Business Type: B2B Software | Annual Organic Revenue: Was $700K (dropped to $280K) | Market: Mid-market competitive niche

The Challenge

After the traffic drop, the company was panicked. They didn't know what went wrong or how to recover. A previous SEO agency had implemented some questionable practices that may have contributed to the penalty. The site also had accumulated technical debt over years of development.

  • Massive unexplained traffic drop from algorithm update
  • Unclear if manual penalty or algorithm change impact
  • Previous SEO work of questionable quality
  • Accumulated technical issues from years of development
  • No clear recovery strategy or prioritization
  • Low morale from sudden revenue loss

Comprehensive Audit & Recovery Strategy

Deep Technical Audit
We conducted a complete technical SEO audit identifying crawl errors, indexing issues, duplicate content problems, broken redirects, and performance issues. Found 47 critical issues.

Content Quality Analysis
We reviewed all previously optimized content and found thin pages, keyword stuffing, and content quality issues that likely contributed to the traffic drop.

Backlink Profile Review
We analyzed backlinks and found some low-quality, unnatural link patterns from previous SEO work. Disavowed approximately 200 questionable backlinks.

Systematic Recovery Plan
We created a prioritized recovery roadmap: fix technical issues first, improve content quality, rebuild clean backlink profile, and implement ongoing monitoring.

Monthly Progress & Monitoring
We implemented weekly tracking and monthly optimization cycles, adjusting strategy based on ranking changes and Google Search Console data.
